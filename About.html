What is Super Position

Can something really be in more than 1 place at a time?

Definition

Physics

When two or more waves cross at a point, the displacement at that point is equal to the sum of the displacements of the individual waves.

Quantum Mechanics

How a quantum state can be represented as the sum of two or more states. Superposition is the ability of a quantum system to be in multiple states at the same time until it is measured.

What/Why Superposition is Important

Imagine touching the surface of a pond at two different points at the same time. Waves would spread outward from each point, eventually overlapping to form a more complex pattern. This is a superposition of waves. Similarly, in quantum science, objects such as electrons and photons have wavelike properties that can combine and become what is called superposed.

A Test of Omnipresence?

Because the concept is difficult to understand, this essential principle of quantum mechanics is often illustrated by an experiment carried out in 1801 by the English physicist, Thomas Young. Young's double-slit experiment was intended to prove that light consists of waves. Today, the experiment is used to help people understand the way that electrons can act like waves and create interference patterns.
